Bottom line

The wedge is hyperpersonalisation needs boring platform excellence. AIB’s public strategy points to personalised insights in the next-generation app, AI-enabled service, segmentation, modern cloud architecture and enterprise AI adoption. That will not land through use-case ideation alone; it needs governed data products, reusable ML/AI patterns, semantic consistency, monitoring and a practical operating model.

  • For Dee: make the conversation about making data science easier to ship safely — fewer bespoke heroics, more reusable platform rails.
  • For AIB: tie the data platform to app personalisation, customer-first engagement, risk controls and speed-to-value.
  • For AA: position as the team that can turn recommendations into executable Databricks foundations and a first thin vertical slice.

What to avoid

  • Do not make this a generic “Databricks can do AI” pitch. AIB already has platform momentum.
  • Do not lead with more workshops. The likely fatigue point is too much advice and not enough implementation traction.
  • Do not over-personalise from private sources. Keep “personal” to professional working style: consumer analytics, customer understanding, practical delivery.
  • Do not frame incumbent-partner politics in writing. Keep this page clean and public-safe.

Where Advancing Analytics should steer the conversation

1. Hyperpersonalisation readiness sprint

Offer: 2–3 weeks to map the first app-personalisation slice from customer signals to governed activation.

  • Define the data products and decisioning signals.
  • Map Unity Catalog lineage and access controls.
  • Identify where real-time/near-real-time is needed vs batch is enough.
  • Produce a pragmatic delivery backlog, not another strategy pack.

2. Data-science industrialisation

Offer: make AIB data science easier to ship into production on Databricks.

  • MLflow experiment/model tracking pattern.
  • Feature, model and segmentation governance.
  • Model monitoring and drift checks.
  • Reusable template for risk-friendly handover.

3. AI/BI + semantic layer pilot

Offer: show how governed natural-language analytics can support app, customer and product teams safely.

  • Certified customer-domain metrics.
  • Genie/AI-BI trusted question set.
  • Permissions, lineage and answer-quality review.
  • Adoption scorecard tied to analyst bottleneck reduction.

Conversation openers

Platform opener:
“The app ambition is clear: personalised insights customers can trust. Where does that currently get hard — data readiness, governance, productionisation, or proving the value of the recommendations?”
Data-science opener:
“If your team had to put one customer-personalisation model or segmentation capability into production this quarter, what part would slow it down most?”
Non-threatening follow-up:
“We’re not here to re-run an assessment. The useful next step may be a thin slice that proves the platform can support one regulated, measurable personalisation journey end to end.”

Questions to ask Dee

  • “What does ‘personalised insight’ mean in the new app: next-best action, financial wellbeing, product relevance, fraud/risk messaging, or service guidance?”
  • “Which customer datasets are trusted enough for activation, and which still need ownership/quality work?”
  • “Where are models or segments currently getting stuck between data science, engineering, risk and product?”
  • “What would make Databricks feel like a delivery accelerator rather than just infrastructure?”
  • “Would a useful next step be a readiness sprint around one hyperpersonalisation use case, with a delivery backlog and reusable controls?”

Suggested next-step offers

  1. Hyperpersonalisation thin-slice: pick one app journey, define data products/signals, build the governed Databricks path, measure lift and trust.
  2. Platform foundations mobilisation: convert review findings into 30/60/90-day engineering tasks across Unity Catalog, CI/CD, data quality, monitoring and operating model.
  3. Data-science productionisation clinic: Dee + AA working session around MLflow, model lifecycle, monitoring, risk evidence and handover patterns.

Risks / objections to prepare for

  • “We already have the platform built.” Response: “Good — now the question is whether product teams and data science can safely deliver on it repeatedly.”
  • “We have internal capability.” Response: “That’s the point: use AA to unblock and upskill the internal team, not replace it.”
  • “We’ve had enough advisory work.” Response: “Then don’t propose advisory. Propose a slice with artefacts, code patterns and acceptance criteria.”
