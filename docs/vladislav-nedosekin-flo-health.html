What Flo Health does

Flo Health is a consumer women’s-health app used for cycle tracking, fertility, pregnancy, postpartum, menopause and broader reproductive-health education. It turns highly sensitive health and behavioural data into personalised, science-backed insights and product journeys at very large scale.

  • Business model / surface: mobile app with personalised insights, content, reminders and AI-assisted health guidance.
  • Data/AI relevance: strong need for privacy, medical-safety controls, experimentation, governed LLM use, recommendation quality and explainability.
  • Commercial implication for AA: focus on production AI patterns, safety evidence, data governance and trusted self-service analytics — not generic app analytics.

Bottom line

With Vlad, the wedge is production AI platform maturity: not “what can Databricks do?”, but “how do you scale AI delivery in a medically sensitive consumer app without slowing engineers down or weakening trust?”

  • AI platform acceleration: help engineering teams ship faster with governed, reusable Databricks patterns.
  • Medical safety: evaluation, fine-tuning, evidence handling, review workflows and traceability around women’s-health content and assistants.
  • Data democratisation: make Genie / AI-BI credible for PMs, analysts, clinicians and business users without creating a compliance mess.

What to avoid

  • Do not pitch Databricks basics — Flo already has adoption and public customer proof.
  • Do not treat “AI assistant” as a shiny demo. The live issue is medical safety, evaluation and trust.
  • Do not over-index on generic healthtech; anchor on women’s health, privacy, scale and experimentation.
  • Do not ask creepy personal questions. Use professional signals only.

Where Advancing Analytics should steer the conversation

1. Safety-grade AI platform review

Offer: a focused review of Flo’s Databricks AI platform patterns around medical content, Ask Flo, evaluation, lineage and governance.

  • Map LLM workflows to safety controls.
  • Define eval harness + sign-off evidence.
  • Trace source-to-answer / source-to-suggestion lineage.
  • Identify reusable patterns for new women’s-health use cases.

2. Genie / AI-BI hardening

Offer: make natural-language data access reliable enough for product, analytics, medical and business teams.

  • Semantic model and certified questions.
  • Unity Catalog permissions and lineage.
  • Hallucination/ambiguity guardrails.
  • Adoption scorecard: usage, accuracy, avoided analyst bottleneck.

3. Agentic product-delivery sprint

Offer: one thin vertical slice for a governed health/workflow agent, not a toy demo.

  • Tool-use and state pattern.
  • Human-in-the-loop review points.
  • Evaluation and monitoring.
  • Production handover checklist for engineering teams.
