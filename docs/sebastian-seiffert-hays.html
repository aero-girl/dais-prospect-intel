Bottom line

Sebastian should be treated as a technical champion with strategic influence, not a generic executive prospect. The best move is not to pitch “AI transformation”; it is to show a practical path from the current Databricks foundation into two adjacent value pools:

  • Genie / self-service analytics: governed business access to trusted insight for marketing, operations and global analytics teams.
  • Lakebase + agentic delivery: Databricks-native operational state and low-latency application patterns for real agent workflows,.

Commercial posture: architecture-first, deliverables-first, business-value clear. No breathless AI nonsense.

What not to do

  • Do not over-focus on Search & Match if he wants to talk platform direction.
  • Do not pitch “agents” as a demo layer. He needs a production pattern.
  • Do not sell Genie as a chatbot. Position it as governed semantic access to Hays’ operating data.
  • Do not bury him in Databricks product slogans. Show where each component fits.

Where Advancing Analytics should steer the conversation

1. Genie / self-service analytics

Positioning: open up trusted business access without breaking governance.

  • Start with a Marketing Genie Space.
  • Curate datasets via Unity Catalog.
  • Define business semantics and example questions.
  • Measure adoption, accuracy and avoided analyst bottleneck.

Databricks defines Genie Spaces as domain-specific natural-language chat interfaces where users ask questions of data and get SQL, result tables and visualisations; analysts curate spaces with Unity Catalog datasets, example SQL and business terminology. Databricks Genie docs.

2. Lakebase for agentic roadmap

Positioning: agents need state, transactions and low-latency serving — not just analytical tables.

  • Map candidate/client workflow states.
  • Identify where an agent needs durable memory/state.
  • Use Lakebase for operational app-facing components where appropriate.
  • Sync back to lakehouse for audit and analytics.

Databricks describes Lakebase as fully managed Postgres integrated into Databricks for real-time transactional applications, low-latency apps, Unity Catalog sync, and as a state store for AI agents. Databricks Lakebase docs.

3. AA agentic delivery support

Positioning: turn prior mixed partner experience into a practical delivery reset.

  • Offer an agent architecture review.
  • Define reference pattern: tool calls, evaluation, governance, state, hand-off.
  • Build a thin vertical slice on Databricks.
  • Leave Hays with reusable patterns and decision points.

Conversation openers

Architecture-first opener:
“It sounds like Search & Match has forced the right platform questions early. I’d be interested in where you now draw the line between analytical AI, operational agent workflows, and governed business self-service.”
Genie opener:
“If you picked one business area outside the technical data team — say Marketing or global analytics — where would governed natural-language access save the most friction first?”
Agentic opener:
“The trap with agents is building another sidecar app. I’d rather map the production pattern: state, tools, governance, evaluation, and where Lakebase genuinely helps.”

Questions to ask Sebastian

  • “Where is Search & Match proving the Databricks foundation — and where is it exposing platform gaps?”
  • “Which Hays business teams are most ready for self-service analytics if governance is solved?”
  • “What would make Genie credible internally: speed, accuracy, semantic control, lineage, adoption, or support model?”
  • “For agents, where do you want the production pattern to be Databricks-native rather than a sidecar application?”
  • “Do you want the next step to be an architecture decision, a Genie pilot, or a thin agentic vertical slice?”

Suggested next-step offers

  1. Marketing Genie Space in 2–3 weeks: curated dataset, semantic instructions, example questions, validation pack, adoption metrics.
  2. Agentic architecture workshop: one half-day mapping use cases to Databricks-native components, Lakebase fit, governance and delivery pattern.
  3. Production agent thin slice: one bounded workflow with state, tool invocation, human hand-off, evaluation and operational telemetry.

Risks / objections to prepare for

  • “Search & Match is already crowded with partners.” Response: “Agreed — we’re not trying to crowd it. We’re looking at the reusable platform patterns that come out of it.”
  • “Genie will hallucinate or confuse business users.” Response: “That’s why the pilot starts with curated semantics, approved datasets and validation criteria.”
  • “We’ve already tried agents.” Response: “Then the value is precisely in a production reference pattern, not another prototype.”
